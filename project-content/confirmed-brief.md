# Confirmed working brief

Last updated: 2026-07-13
Status: Working source; requires formal organization approval before publication.

## Name and history

The official name is **Empower Mind Initiative**. **Empower Mind** may be used as a short name after
the full name has been introduced.

Empower Mind Initiative was founded in November 2022.

## Focus

Empower Mind promotes mental health, psychological well-being, and social resilience among adolescents, young people, and other vulnerable populations.

## Geographic scope

Malawi and beyond.

The supplied feedback confirms work across Zomba and Machinga districts. The organizational profile
also identifies Zomba District as the pilot context, the Southern Region as the current regional
context, and national reach as the ambition. Office and programme-specific locations remain missing.

## Confirmed reach

Stakeholder feedback supplied on 2026-07-13 states that Empower Mind Initiative has reached more
than 5,000 adolescents and young people across Zomba and Machinga districts through a growing
network of Mental Health Ambassadors in five university chapters and secondary schools.

## Challenge areas named in the brief

- Depression
- Anxiety
- Suicide risk
- Substance use
- Trauma
- Psychological stress linked to education
- Psychological stress linked to employment
- Climate shocks
- Social inequality

These terms must be handled responsibly and should not be converted into prevalence claims without sources.

## Vision

To be a leading youth-focused organization shaping mentally healthy, resilient, and empowered generations while supporting vulnerable populations in Malawi and beyond.

This wording is confirmed in the January 2026 organizational profile.

## Mission

To improve the mental health and psychosocial well-being of adolescents, youth, and vulnerable communities in Malawi and beyond through accessible services, community empowerment, research, and advocacy.

This wording is confirmed in the January 2026 organizational profile.

## Working objective

To promote holistic mental health, psychological well-being, and social resilience among adolescents, youth, and vulnerable populations.

This wording requires confirmation and may belong in a broader objectives list.
