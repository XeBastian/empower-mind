# Content and voice

## Voice

Write with calm authority, warmth, and specificity.

Prefer:

- Plain English.
- Active voice.
- Concrete verbs.
- Short, meaningful headings.
- Human descriptions of systems-level work.
- “Young people” over bureaucratic labels unless a technical context requires them.

Avoid:

- “We are passionate about…”
- “Creating a brighter future.”
- “Transforming lives one person at a time.”
- “Together, we can make a difference.”
- “Our holistic approach” without explaining the approach.
- Excessive “empower,” “impact,” “journey,” “change,” and “resilience” repetition.
- Savior narratives or language that defines communities by vulnerability alone.

## Content integrity

Every factual content item must be one of:

- `confirmed` — provided or approved by Empower Mind.
- `provisional` — working copy requiring approval.
- `missing` — known requirement with no source content.
- `retired` — no longer current and not to be published.

Do not turn provisional statements into hard facts.

## Heading style

Headings should carry meaning, not merely label sections.

Weak: “Our Programs”  
Stronger: “How young people shape the response”

Weak: “Our Impact”  
Stronger: “What changes—and how we know”

Use the stronger form only when the supporting content genuinely substantiates it.
