# Visual language

## Character

The design should feel:

- Calm, human, editorial, and assured.
- Youth-aware without becoming childish.
- Rooted in Malawi without using decorative cultural shorthand.
- Contemporary without chasing interface trends.
- Warm enough for community work and rigorous enough for research and advocacy.

## Starting art direction

This is a provisional direction until brand assets are supplied:

- Warm off-white canvas rather than sterile white.
- Deep near-black ink for authority and readability.
- A restrained living green as the primary accent.
- A warm clay or sun-faded amber as a secondary editorial accent.
- Occasional soft neutral fields for pacing, not a rainbow of program colors.

Use `Source Sans 3` for interface and body text and `Source Serif 4` selectively for editorial headlines or pull statements. Self-host fonts when licensing and assets allow; otherwise use a careful system fallback.

## Layout rules

- Use a strong content grid and intentional asymmetry.
- Alternate quiet and dense sections to create rhythm.
- Use generous whitespace, but do not make mobile pages feel empty.
- Let photography breathe; avoid decorating every image with overlays.
- Use full-bleed moments sparingly.
- Vary section composition according to meaning.
- Keep line lengths readable: approximately 45–75 characters for body copy.
- Avoid endless centered headings and identical three-column rows.

## Anti-template rules

Never default to:

- Generic blue NGO branding.
- Gradient mesh backgrounds.
- Glass cards.
- Floating abstract blobs.
- Icon-led feature grids.
- Over-rounded everything.
- Stock illustrations of brains, heads, hearts, or puzzle pieces.
- A hero containing a slogan, two buttons, a smiling stock photo, and decorative statistics.
- Repeating card patterns for every content type.

## Design test

Remove the logo and organization name. The page should still feel intentionally designed, but it must not look interchangeable with a SaaS landing page or another NGO template.
