# Assets and photography

## Photography standard

Use authentic, consented, documentary-style photography connected to Empower Mind's real work.

Seek a balanced library:

- Young people participating, leading, learning, creating, or speaking.
- Facilitators and community spaces.
- Contextual environmental details.
- Research, advocacy, and systems work.
- Quiet portraits only where consent and purpose are clear.
- Images showing agency, relationships, and everyday life—not only service delivery.

## Never use

- AI-generated people.
- Staged “sad person holding head” imagery.
- Identifiable vulnerable participants without documented permission.
- Images that expose sensitive locations or circumstances.
- Decorative photos unrelated to the adjacent claim.
- Generic Western therapy-room imagery as a substitute for Malawian context.

## Asset handling

- Store original source, rights/consent status, photographer credit, date, and caption in an asset register.
- Generate responsive derivatives.
- Preserve focal-point metadata where crops differ.
- Do not enlarge low-resolution source images.
- Do not ship placeholder stock photography to production.
